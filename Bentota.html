Bentota turtle hatchery

In Bentota, Sri Lanka, there is a well-known institution for turtle protection called the Bentota Turtle Hatchery. It acts as a key center for the preservation, treatment, and study of sea turtles, promoting public understanding of the need for their conservation.
A group of dedicated volunteers and environmentalists work hard to protect sea turtles and their habitats at the Bentota Turtle Hatchery. The hatchery places a lot of emphasis on protecting nesting sites, running the hatchery, educating the general public, and undertaking research projects.

Here are some activities and initiatives carried out at the Bentota Turtle Hatchery:

  1. Nesting Site Protection: The hatchery identifies and monitors nesting sites along the nearby beaches, implementing measures to protect the nests from natural and human threats. This helps ensure the safe incubation and hatching of turtle eggs.
  2. Hatchery Management: The facility collects sea turtle eggs from vulnerable nests and transfers them to the hatchery for safe incubation. Once the hatchlings emerge, they are cared for until they reach a suitable size and strength for release into the ocean.
  3. Rehabilitation and Rescue: The Bentota Turtle Hatchery provides a safe haven for injured, sick, or stranded sea turtles. These turtles receive necessary medical attention and rehabilitation before being released back into their natural habitat.
  4. Public Engagement and Education: The hatchery plays a vital role in educating visitors, local communities, and schools about the importance of sea turtle conservation. They conduct educational programs, guided tours, and awareness campaigns to promote responsible behavior towards turtles and their habitats.
  5. Research and Data Collection: The facility actively participates in research projects to gather valuable data on sea turtle biology, behavior, and migration patterns. This research helps in formulating effective conservation strategies and contributes to broader scientific knowledge.


The Bentota Turtle Hatchery offers visitors the chance to see sea turtles at various stages of development, learn about their conservation requirements, and get up close and personal with these amazing animals. The hatchery provides a distinctive and instructive experience that shows the difficulties sea turtles confront and the precautions being taken to safeguard them.
For marine turtles in Sri Lanka, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a ray of hope. By working tirelessly, they hope to secure a bright future for these magnificent species and motivate others to take up the cause of marine conservation.
