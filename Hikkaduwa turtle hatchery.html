Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Centre

Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a popular conservation project located in Hikkaduwa, a coastal town in Sri Lanka. The hatchery is dedicated to the preservation and protection of sea turtles, as well as raising awareness about their conservation.

Description

Near the town of Hikkaduwa, there is a sea turtle hatchery and rescue center. Some of the endangered turtle species in the ecosystem are managed by a center. It is merely one of numerous turtle hatcheries along the country's southern coast. It began as a community initiative to help maintain the south coast of Sri Lanka's biodiversity, but it is now a fully-fledged hatchery and rescue facility with committed teams that work around the clock to study, look after, and protect rare turtle species.

The primary goal of the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is to protect sea turtle eggs from predation and provide a safe environment for the hatchlings until they are ready to be released into the ocean. The hatchery collects sea turtle eggs from vulnerable nesting sites along the coast and carefully incubates them in a controlled environment.

Once the eggs hatch, the hatchlings are kept at the hatchery for a brief period to ensure their health and well-being. During this time, visitors to the hatchery can observe the tiny turtles and learn about their life cycle, threats they face, and conservation efforts. The hatchery also provides information on responsible turtle tourism and the importance of preserving the marine ecosystem.

Visitors to the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery can participate in the release of the hatchlings into the ocean. This hands-on experience allows visitors to witness the turtles' journey back to their natural habitat and contribute to their survival. Releasing the hatchlings at the appropriate time and location increases their chances of survival and helps maintain a healthy sea turtle population.
